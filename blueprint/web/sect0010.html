Definition 5.17 Open subsets and interior
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.ClosureInterior.OpenSubsets_4_19
  • LeanTopology.ClosureInterior.interior_4_19

The interior of a set is defined as the union of all open subsets it contains. In symbols,

\[ \operatorname {int}(A) = \bigcup \{ U\subseteq A \mid U \text{ open}\} . \]

\[ U \text{ open } \iff U=\operatorname {int}(U) \iff U\subseteq \operatorname {int}(U). \]

The interior operator satisfies the standard identities

\[ \operatorname {int}(X)=X, \qquad \operatorname {int}(A)\subseteq A, \qquad \operatorname {int}(A\cap B)=\operatorname {int}(A)\cap \operatorname {int}(B), \qquad \operatorname {int}(\operatorname {int}(A))=\operatorname {int}(A), \]

and abstract interior operators generate topologies in a fully packaged existence-and-uniqueness form.
