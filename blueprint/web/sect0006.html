4 Bases, Subbases, and Countability

Definition 4.1 Topological bases
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.Basis.IsTopologicalBasis_3_1

A family \(\mathcal{B}\) of subsets of \(X\) is a basis for a topology \(\mathcal{O}\) if every basis element is open and every open set is locally refined by some basis element. Equivalently,

\[ \mathcal{B}\subseteq \mathcal{O}, \qquad \forall U\in \mathcal{O}\ \forall x\in U\ \exists B\in \mathcal{B}, \quad x\in B\subseteq U. \]
Proposition 4.2 Union description of bases
✓
#

Uses

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.Basis.topologicalBasis_iff_sUnion_3_2

A family is a basis if and only if every open set is a union of members of that family.

Proof ▶

Assume first that \(\mathcal{B}\) is a basis and let \(U\) be open. For each \(x\in U\), choose a basis element \(B_x\) such that \(x\in B_x\subseteq U\). The union of all these \(B_x\) clearly contains every point of \(U\), and every \(B_x\) lies inside \(U\), so this union is exactly \(U\).

Conversely, suppose every open set is a union of basis elements from \(\mathcal{B}\), and also \(\mathcal{B}\subseteq \mathcal{O}\). If \(x\in U\) with \(U\) open, then writing \(U\) as such a union shows that \(x\) belongs to one chosen basis element \(B\subseteq U\). This is precisely the local refinement condition.

Definition 4.17 Finite intersections and topological subbases
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.Basis.finiteIntersections_3_13
  • LeanTopology.Basis.IsTopologicalSubbasis_3_13

Given a family \(\mathcal S\), one forms the family of its nonempty finite intersections. A subbasis is then a family whose finite intersections form a basis. Thus one first considers

\[ \operatorname {FinInt}(\mathcal S) = \left\{ \bigcap _{i=1}^n S_i \mid n\ge 1,\ S_i\in \mathcal S\right\} , \]

and then asks that \(\operatorname {FinInt}(\mathcal S)\) be a basis.
