2.1 Euclidean-space preliminaries

Proposition 2.1 Cauchy–Schwarz inequality
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.EuclideanSpaceTopology.cauchy_schwarz_0_1

The Euclidean dot product on \(\mathbb {R}^n\) satisfies the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality. In particular,

\[ |\langle x,y\rangle | \le \| x\| \, \| y\| . \]

This is one of the analytic inputs used to organize the later metric structure on Euclidean space.

  • LeanTopology.EuclideanSpaceTopology.norm_triangle_0_2

The Euclidean norm on \(\mathbb {R}^n\) satisfies the triangle inequality. That is,

\[ \| x+y\| \le \| x\| +\| y\| . \]

Consequently it induces the standard Euclidean metric.

Definition 2.4 Euclidean open balls
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.EuclideanSpaceTopology.openBall_eq_metric_ball_0_4
  • LeanTopology.EuclideanSpaceTopology.openBall_mono_0_4

The repository works with Euclidean open balls and records their compatibility with the ambient metric-ball notation, together with their monotonicity in the radius. Equivalently, the discussion centers on the family

\[ B(x,r):=\{ y\in \mathbb {R}^n \mid d(x,y){\lt}r\} , \]

together with the implication

\[ r_1\le r_2 \implies B(x,r_1)\subseteq B(x,r_2). \]
Proposition 2.5 Continuity via balls
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.EuclideanSpaceTopology.continuousAt_iff_ball_0_5

A map between Euclidean spaces is continuous at a point if and only if every target ball around the image contains the image of a sufficiently small source ball around the point. That is, for \(f : \mathbb {R}^n \to \mathbb {R}^m\) and \(a\in \mathbb {R}^n\),

\[ f \text{ is continuous at } a \iff \forall \varepsilon {\gt}0\ \exists \delta {\gt}0\ \forall x, \quad d(x,a){\lt}\delta \implies d\bigl(f(x),f(a)\bigr){\lt}\varepsilon . \]
Definition 2.6 Euclidean open sets
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.EuclideanSpaceTopology.isOpenEuclidean_iff_isOpen_0_6

Open subsets of Euclidean space are described using the local ball condition, and this notion is certified to agree with mathlib’s standard predicate IsOpen. In symbols,

\[ U \text{ open } \iff \forall x\in U\ \exists r{\gt}0, \quad B(x,r)\subseteq U. \]
Proposition 2.7 Basic Euclidean open-set calculus

The Euclidean development includes the basic open-set rules: balls are open, both \(\varnothing \) and \(\mathbb {R}^n\) are open, finite intersections of open sets are open, arbitrary unions of open sets are open, and continuity can be characterized by openness of preimages. In particular, it establishes

\[ \begin{aligned} & B(x,r) \text{ is open}, & & \varnothing ,\mathbb {R}^n \text{ are open},\\ & U_1,U_2 \text{ open } \implies U_1\cap U_2 \text{ open}, & & \{ U_i\} _{i\in I} \text{ open } \implies \bigcup _{i\in I}U_i \text{ open}. \end{aligned} \]

along with the preimage formulation of continuity.
