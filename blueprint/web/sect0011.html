5.4 Boundary and geometric examples

Definition 5.21 Boundary
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • LeanTopology.ClosureInterior.boundary_4_27

The boundary of a set is defined from closure and interior in the standard way. Equivalently,

\[ \partial A = \overline A \setminus \operatorname {int}(A) = \overline A \cap \overline{A^c}. \]

The boundary is closed, satisfies the identity

\[ \partial A = \overline A \cap \overline{A^c}, \]

and is characterized by the property that every neighborhood of a boundary point meets both \(A\) and \(A^c\).

Example 5.23 Boundary and interior of closed unit disks
✓
#

Lean declarations

The development computes explicitly the interior and boundary of the closed unit disk in \(\mathbb {R}^2\), and of the planar closed unit disk sitting inside \(\mathbb {R}^3\). Thus the file identifies concrete formulas of the shape

\[ \operatorname {int}(\overline{B}(0,1)), \qquad \partial \overline{B}(0,1), \]

both in \(\mathbb {R}^2\) and in the corresponding planar subset of \(\mathbb {R}^3\).
